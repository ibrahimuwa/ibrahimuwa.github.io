# Teaching

I see teaching as **research-led inquiry made accessible**. Every course I have taught — from first-year Python to High Performance Computing — is built around the same loop my own research follows: ask a problem worth solving, design and implement, measure rigorously, and communicate clearly. I have over **6 years of full-time university teaching experience** (Assistant Professor, FAST-NUCES, 2013–2019), 2 years as a UWA Teaching Assistant during my PhD, and ongoing HDR supervision as an Adjunct Research Fellow.

## Teaching philosophy

1. **Outcomes-based design.** Aligned with Washington Accord OBE practice (deployed during my AP tenure at FAST-NUCES) — every assessment maps to a learning outcome and a level of Bloom's taxonomy.
2. **Hands-on first.** I teach systems, parallel computing, and AI by **building them**, on the same hardware students will face professionally — Pawsey Setonix, NVIDIA GPUs, FPGAs, Ouster LiDAR sensors and the Linux command line.
3. **Inclusive scaffolding.** Workshops, labs and live coding pace are designed so students from non-traditional CS backgrounds can succeed without diluting depth for advanced students.
4. **AI &amp; LLM literacy.** I teach students how to use modern LLM-driven workflows (prompt engineering, agentic scripting) **responsibly** — as a research and coding accelerator, not a substitute for understanding.
5. **Real datasets, real impact.** I bring open data from my own research (SWAN, PC-Urban, WA Paddock) into assignments, so students experience the same data-quality, scale and noise issues that working scientists do.
